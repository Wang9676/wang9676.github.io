About
Lana Del Rey, born Elizabeth Woolridge Grant on June 21, 1985, is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer known for her hauntingly cinematic and nostalgic sound. Emerging as a cultural icon in the early 2010s, Del Rey gained widespread recognition with her debut major-label album, "Born to Die" (2012), featuring hits like "Summertime Sadness" and "Video Games." Her music often explores themes of love, tragedy, and the American Dream, drawing inspiration from various musical genres, including pop, rock, and hip-hop. Del Rey's unique vocal style, characterized by its sultry and melancholic undertones, has solidified her status as a captivating and influential figure in the contemporary music landscape. Beyond her musical endeavors, she has also delved into poetry and visual arts, showcasing a multifaceted artistic persona.
